Digital literacy narrative

Digital literacy history

Growing up, my family always owned a clunky computer located in our computer room. I can't say for sure, but I can only assume I started using it around the same time I started reading and writing, so I think around the ripe age of five. In the beginning, I mainly used the computer to play pinball, solitaire and minesweeper. I got the hang of logging in and out of a computer as well as its basic functions, but it wasn't until elementary school that I started to use computers for more educational reasons. I learned how to use Microsoft Office throughout elementary school and have been using it ever since. My 8th grade geography teacher introduced my peers and me to Google Suites, so I've been working within this software for awhile now. In high school, I took photography and learned how to use Adobe photoshop. I was in yearbook club and learned how to use Adobe InDesign. Growing up, I got to know basic skills and programs to succeed in school and life.

Digital literacy shortcomings

When it comes to all things digital, I've usually been able to pick up on it quickly. I got a Facebook in the 5th grade and understood how to operate it within minutes, this process is applicable with all social media. I was the family member to turn to when a phone wasn't working or a laptop was malfunctioning. I wouldn't go as far as saying I was tech savvy, but I know my way around digital technologies. With all this confidence, I decided to try my hand in computer science. I enrolled in a computer science course here at the University of Minnesota, but something about certain coding languages wouldn't click in my brain. I ended up withdrawing from the course because it was that difficult for me. Ever since that course, I have been a little bit afraid of coding languages. I worked a job as a Communications Assistant where I worked with HTML and started to become more and more comfortable with coding. I also had the opportunity to learn DITA through an internship I worked the summer of 2020. If it weren't for my mentor, my learning of DITA would have been a lot different. I hope that with time and practice I become familiar with more and more coding languages because I know how beneficial it could be to be proficient.

Digital literacy now

After taking the course Writing with digital technologies, I was able to increase my digital literacy in a multitude of ways. I was able to expand my skills in:

  • HTML
  • MkDocs
  • DITA
  • Github

Although I came across various challenges while learning new technologies, I was able to expand my skill set and become more aware of how certain technologies function and their implications for the future. When the class was assigned to explore the LavaCon webinar series, I got the opportunity to learn more about DITA and its importance in technical communications. This webinar taught me that DITA is becoming a widespread tool throughout technical communications. Seeing how relevant DITA was, I decided to craft my final project using DITA — increasing my proficiency in DITA and my digital literacy altogether. The following chapter will describe specific projects I worked on this semester that helped to increase my digital literacy.
